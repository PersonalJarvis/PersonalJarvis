# Zettelkasten Method

A note-taking workflow that emphasises atomic notes, dense linking, and the absence of a fixed hierarchy. Each note captures a single idea and earns its value through the explicit connections it carries to other notes.

## Core Principles

- **Atomicity** — one note, one idea. Splitting compound topics into separate notes makes them recombinable later.
- **Linking over filing** — relationships beat folders. A note's location matters less than the notes it points to.
- **Permanent rewriting** — the act of restating ideas in your own words is where understanding compounds.

## Relationship to Other Practices

The method is the spiritual predecessor of the modern [[Personal Knowledge Management]] movement. Its mechanic of "ideas surface through neighbours" is what powers the [[Graph View Visualisation]] in tools like Obsidian. Newer thinkers like Tiago Forte have rebranded the practice as [[Evergreen Notes]], adding emphasis on note maturity over note count.

## Practical Setup

A working slip-box needs three layers: fleeting notes (inbox), literature notes (sources), and permanent notes (your own thinking). All three live in plain [[Markdown as Foundation]] today, but the discipline predates the format by half a century.
