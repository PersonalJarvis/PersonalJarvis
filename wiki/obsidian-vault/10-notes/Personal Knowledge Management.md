# Personal Knowledge Management

The deliberate practice of collecting, processing, and connecting information so that a single human can reuse it across years. PKM is less about storage and more about turning fleeting attention into compoundable understanding.

## Defining Activities

1. **Capture** — get raw signal out of your head and into a trusted place fast.
2. **Distill** — restate the signal in your own words so it becomes searchable later.
3. **Connect** — link the new note to existing notes, creating navigable structure.
4. **Express** — write something new, leaning on what you have already connected.

## Method Variants

Different communities have crystallised the same skeleton into named recipes. The [[Zettelkasten Method]] of Niklas Luhmann was the original; [[Evergreen Notes]] is the Andy Matuschak rework that emphasises maturity over count. Both depend on tooling that makes linking cheap — the breakthrough that [[Markdown as Foundation]] enabled.

## Why People Stick With It

Most knowledge workers re-read the same articles three times. A PKM system pays for itself the moment it surfaces something you would otherwise re-discover. The dopamine of [[Graph View Visualisation]] helps too — seeing your own thinking shape into a network is unexpectedly motivating.
